## Cultural Considerations

### Formality Level
- Use formal "usted" (implied) in official documentation and ACCORD
- Use conversational "tú" (implied) for UI strings and chat messages
- Technical terms maintain professional but accessible tone

### Regional Variations
- Spanish translations use neutral Latin American Spanish that works across regions
- Currency and measurement units adapt to local conventions
- Date formats follow ISO 8601 or local preferences

### Technical Term Handling
- Some technical terms remain in English (API, LLM, Pipeline, OAuth) as they are internationally recognized
- Terms like "Agente" and "Adaptador" are translated as they have clear Spanish equivalents
- Acronyms generally remain unchanged (DMA, PDMA, etc.)

## Translation Principles

1. **Clarity over literalism**: Prioritize clear communication over word-for-word translation
2. **Consistency**: Use the same translation for recurring terms
3. **Natural flow**: Maintain natural Spanish sentence structure and idioms
4. **Technical accuracy**: Preserve technical meaning while being accessible
5. **Cultural appropriateness**: Respect Spanish-speaking cultural norms and communication styles
